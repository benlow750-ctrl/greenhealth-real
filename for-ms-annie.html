Strategy Snapshot

A concise view of GreenHealth Pharmacy‚Äôs positioning through a SWOT analysis and a Pirate Funnel (AAARRR) to show how we attract, convert, and grow customers.

Strengths

  • Green Credit loyalty programme encourages repeat visits (retention).
  • Strong brand differentiation as an eco-friendly pharmacy.
  • Sustainable community approach improves brand awareness and customer loyalty.
  • Unique sustainable sunscreen partner with a nationally recognised company (National Skin Centre) to attract customers.

Weaknesses

  • Prices may be higher due to sustainable sourcing of materials.
  • Limited economies of scale: we are a single-outlet, which means we have less bulk purchasing power compared to Watsons/Guardian (higher costs).
  • Technology dependence: Due to use of services such as loyalty app, e-receipts, click-and-collect. System failures may disrupt operations and trust.
  • Strong sustainability focus may not immediately attract elderly or price-sensitive customers.

Opportunities

  • Growing sustainability interest, especially among Gen Z & Millennials.
    • Singlife‚ÄìSGFIN Sustainable Future Index 2026: 83% of respondents aged 18‚Äì65 feel personal responsibility to reduce environmental harm.
  • Support for green initiatives in new towns like Punggol.
  • Partnership potential with eco-brands & suppliers (e.g., Lush, Axiology).
  • Community/government backing: Enterprise Sustainability Programme (ESP) and Enterprise Development Grant (EDG).

Threats

  • Inflation: Customers may start to prioritise price over sustainability when cost of living increases. Discount chains become more attractive, taking away our customers.
  • If sustainability becomes mainstream in the pharmacy ‚Üí GreenHealth may lose our unique brand differentiation.
  • Sustainable procurement narrows our supplier pool: fewer alternatives, higher disruption risk, and sustainable suppliers can raise prices more easily due to stronger market power.

A Awareness

Sources

  • Sustainability-conscious Gen Z & Millennials (18‚Äì35)
  • Young families in Punggol

How we introduce GreenHealth

  • Instagram/TikTok: short content on refillable sunscreen, disposal, eco-habits, general health advice and fun videos
  • Trusted Brand: Partnered with National Skin Centre and popular health brands
  • Special Feature: Sunscreen refill station acts as a unique hallmark to attract customers

Key metrics

Primary Metrics
  • Social media reach: Monthly impressions across Instagram/TikTok
  • Engagement rate: Likes, shares, comments as % of reach
  • Foot traffic: Daily visitor count (entry counter)
Secondary Metrics
  • Instagram/TikTok follower growth rate (monthly)
  • Website unique visitors per month
  • Refill station visibility mentions (how many people say they noticed it)
A Acquisition

Sources

  • Instagram / TikTok
  • Word-of-mouth in sustainability communities
  • Walk-ins attracted by visible green initiatives

Most valuable channels

  • Social ‚Üí in-store conversion for young adults
  • Telegram channel for promos & updates
  • Cashier counter prompts for sign-ups

Key metrics

Primary Metrics
  • New member registrations: Total member registrations per week
  • Cost per acquisition (CPA): Marketing spend √∑ new customers acquired
  • Channel attribution: % of new customers from each source (social media, walk-in, referral, Telegram)
  • Sign-up conversion rate: New members √∑ total store visitors
Secondary Metrics
  • Telegram channel subscriber growth
A Activation

First key action

  • Sign up for Green Credit loyalty membership

‚ÄúWOW‚Äù moment

  • Rewards for eco-actions (expired med return, e-receipts, reusable bag)
  • Free lifelong-membership signup
  • Exclusive member discounts with GreenHealth partner brands
  • Free sunscreen sample after app registration

Why this works

Customers experience immediate value and impact from this action: saving money, and taking the first step to a sustainable future.

Key metrics

Primary Metrics
  • Membership activation rate: % of sign-ups who complete first sustainability action within 7 days
  • Time to first value: Average hours/days from sign-up to first Green Credit earned
  • Welcome offer redemption rate: % of new members who claim their free tote bag + sunscreen sample
Secondary Metrics
  • Same-day purchase rate (sign up and buy immediately)
R Retention

How we retain customers

  • Green Credit points from purchases and sustainable behaviours
  • Membership tiers encourage more spending for better rewards
  • Telegram updates: promos, tips, new product launches
  • Follow-notifications from membership app: birthday rewards, inactivity reminders, refill prompts
  • Community belonging: branding ourselves as a pharmacy for the neighbourhood, not just another store

Key metrics

Primary Metrics
  • Repeat purchase rate: % of customers making ‚â•2 purchases in 90 days
  • Purchase frequency: Average visits per member per month
  • Active member rate: % of members with at least 1 transaction in last 30 days
  • Churn rate: % of members with no activity in 90 days
  • Cohort retention: % of members who return after 30, 60, 90 days
Secondary Metrics
  • Average Green Credit points earned per active member
  • Membership tier distribution (Bronze/Silver/Gold or however structured)
  • Telegram engagement rate (opens, clicks on promos)
  • App open frequency per user per month
  • Refill frequency for sunscreen customers
R Revenue

How we generate income

Main plan: Stimulate market demand and brand interest so customers choose and stay with GreenHealth.
  • Sale of prescription medicines, OTC products, supplements, skincare, health essentials
  • Signature refillable sunscreen
  • Loyalty-linked sustainability services increase lifetime value

Key metrics

Primary Metrics
  • Monthly revenue
  • Average transaction value (ATV): Mean transaction size per visit
  • Product mix ratio: Refillable/sustainable products vs traditional products as % of revenue
  • Margin per transaction: Average profit after Cost of Goods Sold
Secondary Metrics
  • Refill sunscreen revenue vs full-bottle sales
  • Loyalty redemption rate (points used vs earned)
R Referral

How referrals happen

  • Referral incentives: Bonus Green Credit points for successful referrals
  • Customer advocacy: sharing experiences on social media + community groups

Key metrics

Primary Metrics
  • Referral rate: % of existing customers who successfully refer someone
  • Referred customer sign-ups: New members who used a referral code/link
  • Referral conversion rate: Referred sign-ups √∑ total referral links shared
Secondary Metrics
  • Social media mentions and tags
  • Google/Facebook reviews submitted per month
  • Word-of-mouth attribution (survey: "How did you hear about us?")

Insights on how the project enhanced understanding of pharmacy operations and business models

This project helped me understand how pharmacy operations function in a real-world setting. I learned that a pharmacy must carefully plan its services, products, pricing, and patient engagement strategies. These decisions influence how the pharmacy attracts patients, supports their needs, and remains sustainable over time.

One key insight was product planning. While developing the sustainable sunscreen, we considered more than just the product itself. We evaluated patient needs, pricing, and sustainability impact. The refillable container reduced plastic waste while encouraging repeat purchases, and price comparisons ensured affordability. This showed me that product decisions must balance patient benefit, cost, and long-term viability.

I also learned how pharmacies use multiple channels to reach patients. Awareness was generated through platforms such as Telegram, Instagram, and TikTok, while services were accessed through both physical and online options like click-and-collect. This demonstrated that pharmacies must plan both how patients discover services and how easily they can access them.

Another important insight was how loyalty programmes support retention. The membership programme rewarded purchases, medication returns, sunscreen refills, and other sustainable actions, encouraging repeat visits and long-term relationships. I realised these systems must provide clear value and remain simple to use to maintain engagement.

Overall, this project helped me see that pharmacy operations involve more than dispensing medicines. A pharmacy must strategically align products, services, pricing, and patient engagement to meet patient needs while operating sustainably.
