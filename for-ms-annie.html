Strengths

Weaknesses

  • Technology dependence: Due to use of services such as loyalty app, e-receipts, click-and-collect. System failures may disrupt operations and trust.

Insights on how the project enhanced understanding of pharmacy operations and business models

This project helped me understand how pharmacy operations function in a real-world setting. I learned that a pharmacy must carefully plan its services, products, pricing, and patient engagement strategies. These decisions influence how the pharmacy attracts patients, supports their needs, and remains sustainable over time.

One key insight was product planning. While developing the sustainable sunscreen, we considered more than just the product itself. We evaluated patient needs, pricing, and sustainability impact. The refillable container reduced plastic waste while encouraging repeat purchases, and price comparisons ensured affordability. This showed me that product decisions must balance patient benefit, cost, and long-term viability.

I also learned how pharmacies use multiple channels to reach patients. Awareness was generated through platforms such as Telegram, Instagram, and TikTok, while services were accessed through both physical and online options like click-and-collect. Another important insight was how loyalty programmes support retention. The membership programme rewarded purchases, medication returns, sunscreen refills, and other sustainable actions, encouraging repeat visits and long-term relationships. I realised these systems must provide clear value and remain simple to use to maintain engagement.

Overall, this project helped me see that pharmacy operations involve more than dispensing medicines. A pharmacy must strategically align products, services, pricing, and patient engagement to meet patient needs while operating sustainably.

Insights on how the project enhanced understanding of pharmacy operations and business models

This project helped me understand that pharmacy operations and business models are connected because it made me see the pharmacy as one whole system. A pharmacy is not only a place to dispense medications; it also functions as a retail business and a community health service at the same time. Because of that, decisions cannot be made in isolation. What we promise customers must match what we can deliver, how we reach customers, and whether it is financially sustainable.

Working on the Green Credit membership page made the link between customer relationships, retention, and operations very clear. Although it looks like a rewards programme, each reward is a commitment the pharmacy must deliver consistently to support sustainability practices. For example, rewarding medication returns and sunscreen refills requires a clear workflow for safe handling, refill support, accurate point tracking (e.g., scanning the membership QR code), and smooth redemption.

Green Credit also builds retention through its point system by rewarding repeat engagement and consistent behaviours such as digital-only receipts, reusable bags, refills, and referrals. This showed me that loyalty is created through structured incentives and reliable execution. For customers who care about sustainability, the membership makes our promise tangible by rewarding the behaviours they already value, so the idea feels real and customer-focused.

Another insight I gained was the importance of using the right channels for different purposes. Social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok, together with in-store prompts at the counter, help attract attention and encourage sign-ups. The website provides a credible place to understand the concept, while the mobile app delivers the loyalty experience through point tracking and redemption. This showed me that these channels build on each other, with social media and the website bringing customers in, and the app sustaining engagement.

This project also made me think more about trade-offs. Sustainability still needs planning to stay practical, so partnerships and systems matter. For example, collaborating with the National Skin Centre (NSC) allows GreenHealth to offer a sustainable and credible product at a competitive price, rather than positioning sustainability as something automatically more expensive. In addition, the refill model helps lower ongoing packaging costs while reducing packaging waste, which supports affordability in the long run.

Overall, this project helped me see that a strong pharmacy concept requires alignment across customer needs, daily operations, and financial planning, not just good branding or good ideas.
