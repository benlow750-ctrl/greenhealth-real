Strengths

  • Green Credit loyalty programme encourages repeat visits (retention).
  • Strong brand differentiation as an eco-friendly pharmacy.
  • Sustainable community approach improves brand awareness and customer loyalty.
  • Unique sustainable sunscreen partner with a nationally recognised company (National Skin Centre) to attract customers.

Weaknesses

  • Prices may be higher due to sustainable sourcing of materials.
  • Limited economies of scale: we are a single-outlet, which means we have less bulk purchasing power compared to Watsons/Guardian (higher costs).
  • Technology dependence: Due to use of services such as loyalty app, e-receipts, click-and-collect. System failures may disrupt operations and trust.
  • Strong sustainability focus may not immediately attract elderly or price-sensitive customers.

  • Growing sustainability interest, especially among Gen Z & Millennials.

Threats

  • Inflation: Customers may start to prioritise price over sustainability when cost of living increases. Discount chains become more attractive, taking away our customers.

Insights on how the project enhanced understanding of pharmacy operations and business models

This project helped me understand that pharmacy operations and business models are connected, and business model thinking is useful because it forces you to see the pharmacy as one whole system. A pharmacy is not only a place to dispense medications; it also functions as a retail business and a community health service at the same time. Because of that, decisions cannot be made in isolation. What we promise customers must match what we can deliver, how we reach customers, and whether it is financially sustainable. Working on the Green Credit membership page made the link between customer relationships and operations very clear. On the surface, it looks like a rewards programme, but each reward is also a commitment to support sustainability practices that the pharmacy must be able to deliver consistently. For example, if we reward medication returns and sunscreen refills, we need a proper workflow for handling returns safely, supporting refill processes, tracking points accurately (e.g., scanning the membership QR code), and making redemption smooth. To make the membership effective, the workflow and tracking systems need to be consistent so customers have a smooth experience. The membership page also helped me understand how business models build retention. Green Credit encourages repeat engagement not only by giving ‚Äúdiscounts‚Äù, but by creating a routine where customers benefit from consistent behaviours (digital-only receipts, reusable bags, refills, referrals). This showed me that customer retention is designed through structured incentives and a consistent experience. Another insight I gained was the importance of using the right channels for different purposes. GreenHealth does not rely only on the website. By leveraging social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok, and using in-store prompts at the counter, we can attract attention and encourage sign-ups more effectively. The website supports this by providing a clear and credible place for customers to understand the concept, while the mobile app delivers the loyalty experience through point tracking, redemption, digital receipts, and referrals. This showed me that the channels build on each other. Channels bring customers in, and the app sustains engagement by making rewards convenient and consistent. Finally, this project made me understand trade-offs more clearly, especially for sustainability. Sustainability-focused decisions can increase cost, so the business model must justify these costs in a way customers accept. That is why the membership strategy matters; it adds value through rewards and exclusive benefits instead of simply increasing product prices. This helped me see that sustainability has to be supported by a realistic plan, not just a message. Overall, this project helped me see that a strong pharmacy concept requires alignment across customer needs, daily operations, and financial planning, not just good branding or good ideas.
